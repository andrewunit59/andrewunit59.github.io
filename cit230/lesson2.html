    The Rule of Thirds

    The Rule of Thirds is a rule in photography where an image is divided evenly into thirds, both horizontally and vertically. The subject of the image is located on or near these lines, and is not in the center. The purpose of this placement is to draw the viewer's eye away from the center, and to other parts of the image.
